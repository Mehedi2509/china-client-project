Pre-terminated wire harness

We offer pre-terminated harnesses designed for tough situations and rapid use. These harnesses are made with top-quality materials and extra care.

Product Introduction

A pre-terminated harness is not just a pile of wires. It is a carefully designed and built wire set. Skilled workers and machines work together to make it exact and safe. First, each colored wire is cut and stripped to the correct size. Then the ends, such as spade, blade, pin, or bullet, are pressed tightly with steady force so they will not come loose. These crimps are strong and reliable. The ends are also coated with tin or gold to stop rust and to last longer. For example, many female spade ends are made from tin-plated brass and tested to meet safety rules. After this, the wires are grouped into one neat and tidy bundle. Each color is chosen for meaning black for live, white for return, green for ground so workers can spot the right wire immediately without confusion.

Material Recommendation

TE Connectivity Female Crimp Terminals

These female crimp terminals from TE Connectivity are simple, safe, and easy to use. They are often called FASTON female or socket terminals. They let you connect or remove wires very fast without needing extra tools. A widespread size is 0.250” (6.35mm). These parts are made from strong tin-plated brass, which does not rust easily. They are tested and certified for safety (UL/CSA). Small ones can carry about 7 amps of power, while bigger ones carry more. Some have nylon covers that protect against touch. Inline female terminals are also used in many harness jobs. They all give tight and steady joins, which keep the power flowing smoothly. Because of this, they are used in cars, machines, and home devices where safe and strong wiring is needed.

TE Connectivity Male Crimp Terminals

These are the matching male tabs for the female sockets. They are also called FASTON male connectors. They are made of tin-plated brass, which makes them last longer and stay safe from rust. The most used sizes are 0.250” (6.35mm) and 0.187” (4.75mm). One well-known type is the FASTIN-FASTON tab. Smaller 0.187” tabs are used when space is tight or the job is lighter. Some come with insulation covers for extra safety. TE makes both insulated and bare types in many wire sizes. They fit tightly into housings, so they will not loosen with movement or vibration. The closed-barrel crimp style grips wires all around, holding them firm and safe.

Male Open-Barrel Crimp Terminals (Pin Terminals)

These connectors are used when a firm wire hold is needed. They are shaped with small wings that fold around the bare wire and its cover. They are often used in cars, planes, and heavy machines because they do not loosen when things shake. They are usually made of copper alloys and coated with tin, nickel, or gold to make them strong and long-lasting. They come in different shapes, like round pins or flat tabs. Special crimp tools press them into neat “B” or “F” shaped crimps. These crimps make powerful bonds. They fit inside housings to form large, safe, and reliable multi-wire plugs.

Female Open-Barrel Crimp Terminals (Receptacle Terminals)

These connectors are made to join with male open-barrel pins. They are tin-plated copper alloy and grip wires very well. Small wings fold over the wire and its cover to lock it tightly. When placed inside housings, they snap onto the male pins and make a safe and firm join. These connectors do not loosen even in cars or machines that shake a lot. They are certified for safety and used in car ECUs, sensors, and other block plugs where many wires must join.

Female Spade Crimp Terminals (Tin-Plated)

Spade connectors are standard because they are quick to use. These TE FASTON parts can be plain or insulated. They are made of brass with a tin coat, which keeps them strong and stops rust. They come in 187 and 250 series, with small 2.8mm (0.110”) types for light jobs. They slide onto studs, fuse blades, or relay tabs with ease. They are often used for ground wires or power ends. The push-on style makes them very fast to connect or remove, saving time during repair or setup.

Female Blade Crimp Terminals (2.8 mm Series)

Small spade types for tight spots. They fit onto 0.110” male tabs. Even though they are small, they carry enough current for many jobs. Many come with insulation for added safety. They are used in LED lights, small harnesses, home appliances, and telecom devices. They meet global safety rules and are good where space is short, but a safe and strong connection is still needed.

Male Round Pin Crimp Terminals

These are also known as bullet connectors. They are round brass pins, often plated with tin or nickel for strength and rust resistance. The wire is crimped to the pin and then pushed into a matching socket. They come in many sizes, with 0.18” being common. They can be used in housings or as single connectors. They are widely used in lights and add-on parts. They make quick and safe joins and carry high currents. Many are sold in kits, and some have insulation covers. They are strong and last a long time when made from good materials.

Solution

Our pre-terminated harnesses solve wiring problems in a straightforward step. They put many circuits into one neat bundle, so the wires are not messy. Each harness is built to match your needs: correct length, color, and connectors. Because cables are grouped, workers don’t waste time searching for wires or crimping one by one. Red wires with metal ends

Red cables, like +12V or +24V, are used for power. We crimp each red wire with a strong end, such as a spade or bullet. These ends are coated to last long. Each crimp has a firm press and a color sleeve to show the size. This is very important for high-power lines. Even small spade tabs are made from tin-plated brass for extra safety.

White wires with plugs

White wires are usually for return or neutral. We add safe plugs or covers to keep them protected. For example, white PVC wires may have quick-connect plugs that fit tightly. These ready-made ends save time and stop mistakes. Each white wire is clearly marked so anyone can see what it is for.

Bright wires with metal ends

The bright and colorful cables make each line easy to see. Red is live, black is ground, white is neutral, and green is earth. Each end is crimped with a strong tip, such as a spade or a pin. Many tips are made from tin-plated brass that stop rust and wear. This ensures that no loose wire strands are left.
