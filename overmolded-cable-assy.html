Product Introduction

M12 round connectors are plugs that have four or five pins. It has a rounded shape. To connect wires, machines, or tools, you can use them easily. Overmolded cable assemblies are used for many sectors to supply power and signals. These cables and the plugs should be able to resist rust and dirt to work properly. Plastic overmolding is the best and exact process we use to connect the cables perfectly. The hot plastic perfectly keeps the connector inside in this step. For exact bonding, this process is necessary. The plastic completely takes the pressure of the core cable and keeps it safe from tough uses. There is a tool that keeps the connector in the same place when the mold is filled with plastic. After it cools down, it is fully sealed. This process is vital for the cable to work in tough situations.

Material Recommendation

Copper

Good copper wires are key to any cable. We use pure copper with high flow for the best work. This cuts signal loss and heat. It also lasts longer and gives steady power. We use thin strands with a tin coat for flex and safe joins. The wire size is picked to carry the right power and still bend easily. This way, copper wires give both strong power and simple use.

Fiberglass

We use fiberglass rods to make the cable stiff. Fiberglass is hard and stops bending. It keeps the overmolded cable assembly straight near the join and adds life. But it may crack in very cold or hot places. We use it when stiff parts are needed indoors. Fiberglass adds strong support without a significant cost.

PVC

PVC is a low-cost and straightforward choice for cable covers. It is soft, bonds well to PVC jackets, and flows well in molds. For example, a PVC jacket with PVC resin makes a tight seal. PVC is good against wear, oil, and fire. But it can turn stiff in cold weather and is not best for hot weather. We use PVC primarily for indoor and low-cost jobs.

Connector

The joint part is often metal or hard plastic. For M12, the body is nickel brass or steel, which is strong and does not rust. Inside is a plastic ring to hold pins in place. The pins are copper with a gold coating for long life and good flow. The metal shell also stops noise and links to the shield if needed. We pick the body to match the job, like steel for sea work or alloy for light jobs. Each connector is made for safe and long use.

Steps We Apply to Make Overmolded Cable Assembly

Consultation & Design

We learn your needs and check your plans. We pick the best M12 connector and the right cable. Our team makes a 3D model and may print a sample so you can see how it looks and fits. Then we pick the right plastic, like PVC or TPU, and make a complete plan. Every step is shown to you for approval.

Prototype & Tooling

We built a sample with 3D-printed molds to test fit and function. After the tests, we made the final mold with strong steel or aluminum. This mold holds the overmolded cable assembly in place so the overmold forms right each time.

Material Prep

Wires are cut, stripped, and set in the mold. Connectors are fixed and checked. Each wire is tested before moving forward. Nothing goes ahead unless it is correct.

Injection Overmolding

Heated plastic flows around the overmolded cable assembly and connector inside the mold. For soft or small parts, we use low-pressure overmolding to keep them safe. The plastic fills all spaces and makes a hard cover that blocks water and stress.

Cooling & Unmolding

When the plastic gets hardened and the mold is cooled and ready to open, then it opens. The cable is taken out, trimmed, and shaped. Extra boots or rings may be added for strength.

Finishing & Assembly

More support is added if needed. Each overmolded cable assembly is cleaned, labeled, and color-coded. We check each one for a smooth look and good shape.

Testing

Each overmolded cable assembly is tested for power, safety, and signal. Bad ones are removed. Good ones are marked with numbers for tracking. This makes sure only safe cables ship.

Delivery

After all that, the harnesses get carefully packed and shipped to you. They come ready to use with clear marks and colors. You get safe, complete, and long-lasting color-coded overmolded cable assemblies.
