Our Abilities and Standards

We make many types of quick turn wire harnesses for all kinds of needs. Every harness is made to fit precisely what you need, so it works right the first time. We check each harness carefully to ensure it is strong, safe, and ready to last long without trouble.

Supported Harness Types from Simple to Complex

We can make a simple harness with only one wire for a basic job. We can also make large harnesses with many cables, branches, and connectors going in different directions. Some harnesses are built to fit into small, tight spaces where it is hard to work. Others are made to link to various kinds of machines or tools simultaneously. Every design is built to match your project, so it works how you need it to.

Wire Gauges, Covers, and Shielding Choices

There are wires in many sizes, from very thin to very thick. Thin cables are light and work well for smaller jobs, while thick wires are stronger and suitable for heavy-duty work. There are always high quality covers to protect the wires because we add the best quality items to it. We can also add shields around the wires to stop outside signals or noise from causing problems. Every choice is made so the harness works well where it will be used.

Best Connector Types for Strong and Safe Links

The plugs and connectors for each harness are always high quality. Some connectors are round to fit into small spaces. Others are flat for vast areas. Some are shaped in a special way to fit specific machines. Every connector is made to lock in tightly so it stays in place, even after prolonged use. Good connectors keep the harness working safely and reliably. We only use high-quality connectors to ensure they last and give the best results.

Rules, Standards, and Careful Testing

We follow trusted rules like IPC/WHMA-A-620, so every harness is made correctly. Each harness goes through careful testing before it leaves our shop. We check that power flows from one end to the other without breaks. We look for any weak spots or loose wires and fix them.

Support for CAD Files, Drawings, and Reverse Design

We can work from many plans, such as CAD files, printed drawings, or simple hand sketches. If you do not have a plan, we can copy an old harness and make a new one. This process is called reverse design. We carefully study each part of the old harness, then create a new version that works the same or better. This is useful when a harness is no longer made or the original maker is unavailable.
